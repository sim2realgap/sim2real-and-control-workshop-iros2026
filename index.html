Sim2Real and Classical Control: From Rigorous Theory to Data-Driven Robotics

Join our workshop to explore state-of-the-art simulators and new research methodologies at the edge between control and sim-to-real transfer.

IROS 2026 Pittsburgh

About the Workshop

Simulators have served as essential instruments in the establishment of robotics as a tangible reality, and they have typically been one step ahead of real-world implementations. Today, we are in a new era where simulators such as Gazebo, MuJoCo, OpenAI Gym, PyBullet, RoboSumo are becoming part of the robot deployment pipeline, enabling control and perception policies trained in simulation to be deployed on real robots. This methodology is known as sim-to-real (sim2real) transfer. In a complementary direction, the community is increasingly leveraging real-world data to refine simulators, a process often referred to as real-to-sim (real2sim). In such a scenario, it is unclear to what extent sim2real and real2sim represent new methodologies or prior arts well-known in the state-of-the-art as adaptive control and system identification.

Targeted Topics

  • Sim-to-Real (Sim2Real) Transfer in Robotics
  • Real-to-Sim (Real2Sim) System Identification
  • Adaptive and Robust Control
  • Classical Control vs Learning-Based Control
  • Domain Randomization & Domain Adaptation
  • Reinforcement Learning for Robotic Systems
  • Physics-Based Simulation Tools (Gazebo, MuJoCo, PyBullet, etc.)
  • Data-Driven Modeling and Hybrid Control Architectures
  • Benchmarking and Evaluation Methodologies
